## What problem does Lcurve solve?

Many problems in science and engineering require recovering an unknown quantity `x` from indirect, noisy measurements `b` through a forward model `A`. When `A` is ill-conditioned or singular — as it often is in image deblurring, geophysical inversion, medical imaging, and signal processing — naive least-squares solutions amplify measurement noise and produce useless results. These are called **ill-posed inverse problems**.

The core challenge is not just solving `A*x ≈ b`, but choosing *how much* to regularize: too little and the solution is dominated by noise; too much and it is over-smoothed and biased. Lcurve automates this choice.

## Tikhonov regularization

Tikhonov regularization replaces the naive least-squares problem with a penalized one:

```
x(λ) = argmin  |A*x - b|² + λ * |L*x|²
```

The regularization matrix `L` encodes prior knowledge about the solution (e.g., `L = I` for 0th-order, or a difference matrix for smoothness), and `λ ≥ 0` controls the trade-off between data fidelity and regularization. Lcurve solves this family of problems for any set of `λ` values you supply, or finds the best `λ` automatically.

## The L-curve criterion

The **L-curve** is a log-log plot of the residual norm `log|A*x(λ) - b|²` versus the regularization norm `log|L*x(λ)|²`, traced over a range of `λ` values. It typically has a characteristic L-shape:

- The **vertical part** (small `λ`): data is fit well but the solution is noisy.
- The **horizontal part** (large `λ`): the solution is smooth but poorly fits the data.
- The **corner** (the "knee"): the optimal balance point, corresponding to the `λ` of maximum curvature.

The knee of the L-curve is a reliable, parameter-free heuristic for selecting `λ` without requiring knowledge of the noise level — unlike methods such as discrepancy principle or GCV.

## Why Lcurve?

Most L-curve implementations estimate curvature using finite differences over a discrete grid of `λ` values, which introduces numerical errors and requires a dense, carefully-chosen grid. Lcurve computes the curvature of the log-L-curve **analytically** — the exact derivative is derived symbolically and evaluated in closed form for each `λ`. This means:

- Curvature is exact at any single `λ`, not an approximation.
- The knee-finding algorithm does not depend on grid density.
- Osculating circle centers are computed alongside curvature, enabling precise geometric visualization.

The automatic knee-finding performs an iterative refinement search (starting from `λ ∈ 10^(−20:20)`) that zooms in on the maximum-curvature region, converging to a reliable optimal `λ` without manual tuning.
